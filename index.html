Short Bio - I am full professor of Statistics at the University of Salerno and a faculty member of the NSE graduate school of the University of Naples "Federico II". I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from University College London (UCL, UK), and my Master of Science (M.Sc.) at the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn, USA). I am broadly interested in the intersection of statistics and computational data science, with applications to machine learning and artificial intelligence. Over the years, I have taught both undergraduate and graduate courses in inference, probability, computational and learning methods, and econometrics. For more information, see my Curriculum Vitae.

Software
OTRIMLE: robust model-based clustering

OTRIMLE is an R package for robust cluster analysis allowing for outliers and noise. The data are modelled by a mixture of Gaussian distributions and a noise component. Download from CRAN.

RSC: robust and sparse correlation matrix

RSC computes an estimate of the correlation matrix resistant to noise and outliers, applying adaptive thresholding for sparsity. Download from CRAN.

hyQSAR: hybrid quantitative structure–activity relationship modelling

hyQSAR performs hybrid QSAR modelling by integrating structural properties and MOA information. Download via GitHub.

RCPS: robust clustering for patient subtyping

RCPS performs robust clustering for patient subtyping that guarantees optimal separation in terms of survival patterns. Download via GitHub.
